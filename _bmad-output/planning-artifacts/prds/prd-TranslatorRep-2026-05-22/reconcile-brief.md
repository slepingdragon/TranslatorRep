# Brief Reconciliation — TranslatorRep PRD

## Overall verdict

The PRD captures the Brief's load-bearing qualitative content with unusual fidelity — the emotional core, the asymmetric translation load, the "he built this for them" signal, the deep-conversation success criterion, the Sundanese-gap honesty, and the polished-UI bar all survived the FR structure intact. Vision, Personas/JTBD, UJ-3, §5 Quality, §7 Aesthetic, and SM-7 each carry forward specific Brief language rather than paraphrasing it away. A small number of qualitative claims from the Brief landed only partially (the "fast" timeline, the publish-for-others-maybe vision arc), and the PRD adds a couple of framings the Brief itself does not state but which align with Bania's decision-log choices.

## Gaps (qualitative content the FR structure dropped)

- **The "fast" speed constraint is missing from the PRD body.** Brief: *"Bania wants this fast — the architecture and stack choices have already been optimized for build speed… No further compression possible without sacrificing the polish bar."* The PRD has no Timeline section and no statement that build speed is a constraint. It mentions stack/free-tier choices in §8 and §10 but not the *reason* (speed) or the timebox (~1 week ramp + 4–6 weeks). This should land in either §1 Vision or a new "Timeline" / "Constraints" subsection.
- **The vision arc "publish for others maybe, but us first" is absent.** Brief Vision §: *"publishing it on App/Play Stores for other couples in similar situations is a possibility, not a plan. Bania wants this for them first, fast."* The PRD's §1 Vision ends at "tool to eliminate the translation labor she carries alone today" and §10.2 lists "Public distribution" as a v2 candidate without the "possibility, not a plan" qualifier or the "earned by sustained use" framing. This is a meaningful nuance — the PRD reads as if public distribution is a normal v2 roadmap item, whereas the Brief frames it as conditional on the experience earning it.
- **The "Months 2–3: v2 earns its place permanently" framing is dropped.** Brief: *"This is the version where the app earns its place permanently."* The PRD treats v2 Sundanese as a sequenced feature (§10.2) and preserves "not optional" but loses the *why* — that v1 alone doesn't permanently justify the build because her code-switching pattern is daily. The "not optional" language is preserved; the "earns its place" framing is not.
- **The "WhatsApp stays for everything else" line is partially captured but loses its quiet force.** Brief: *"WhatsApp stays for everything else."* The PRD captures the functional equivalent in §2.2 ("WhatsApp continues to host everything else") and §9 ("Faces stay on WhatsApp"), so the content is present, but the deliberate restraint in the Brief — that this app is intentionally narrow and not trying to replace anything — is more diffuse in the PRD.
- **The Brief's "thousands of micro-translations… invisible labor isn't sustainable indefinitely" texture is compressed.** Brief: *"Over a 30-minute call she's done thousands of micro-translations and dozens of 'wait, the right English word is…' moments. He's just had a conversation."* The PRD §1 carries "translation labor she carries alone today" and §2.2 Emotional carries "asymmetric translation labor," which is faithful but flatter. Not a problem — the PRD points to the Brief as the source — but worth noting the texture is in the Brief, not the PRD.

## Misalignments (PRD says something the Brief doesn't support)

- **None substantive.** I looked specifically for: a fabricated competitive moat (Brief explicitly omitted "what makes this different" — PRD honors this, no "Why now" / "Why us" section invented), a softened Sundanese gap (PRD §10.2 has a NOTE FOR PM explicitly preserving the harder framing per decision log — correctly aligned), or any claim that this is a market product (PRD §9 Non-Goals leads with "Not a market product in v1" — aligned with Brief §2 and §6).
- **Minor framing addition (not a misalignment, but worth flagging):** PRD §1 Vision adds *"translated with attention to the discourse particles, slang, and code-switching patterns that carry the emotional content of conversational Indonesian."* The Brief does not say this — it says only "translated in under three seconds." This is a *legitimate* PRD-level synthesis from the Domain Research, but it does inject a quality-framing into the Vision that the Brief itself does not make. Not contradictory; just additive. Worth knowing it came from DR, not the Brief.
- **The "≥3x per week for the first month" metric is preserved but reframed.** Brief calls this "the metric that matters"; PRD SM-1 calls it "the metric that matters most" and adds parenthetical FR-linkage. Aligned, not a misalignment, but the PRD's SM-1 explanation ("if they don't actually use it instead of WhatsApp-without-translation, the technical success is wasted") closely paraphrases the Brief — good.

## Captured-well (positive: this Brief content the PRD did right)

- **Emotional core is preserved across multiple PRD locations.** §1 Vision carries "long deep conversations they already have stop having quiet miscommunication undercurrents" almost verbatim from the Brief. §2.2 JTBD-Emotional captures asymmetric labor. §2.1 Persona explicitly carries the "he built it for them" signal: *"Will appreciate the tool both for what it does AND for the signal that he built it for them."* This is exactly the load-bearing signal the FR structure usually drops, and it's intact.
- **UJ-3 is the emotional climax of the PRD and matches the Brief's qualitative success criterion.** The Brief's qualitative win — *"a long, deep conversation where no miscommunication happens"* — appears as the explicit climax of UJ-3 and again as SM-7 (Qualitative win-condition). Two independent landing places, both faithful. Including the family-vulnerability moment in UJ-3 is a strong illustrative choice that grounds the abstract success signal.
- **Sundanese gap honesty is preserved with the harder framing.** PRD §10.2 carries the explicit "This is a real and visible gap" language, the "v2 is not optional — just sequenced after v1 ships" claim, and a NOTE FOR PM locking in the harder framing per Bania's decision-log choice over the DR-softened version. This is exactly correct per the user's instruction.
- **"What makes this different" is correctly omitted.** Brief deliberately drops the standard "competitive moat" framing because this is a personal app. PRD has no "Why now / Why us / Competitive landscape" section. No fabricated moat. Correctly honored.
- **Polished-UI bar is fully captured and elaborated.** Brief: *"Polished, native UI from day one."* PRD §7 Aesthetic and Tone gives this a full section — monochrome glass, centered layout, anti-references (no enterprise chips, no AI sparkle clichés), visual touchstones (iOS 26 Control Center, Bear, Linear). Goes beyond the Brief in specificity, which is appropriate for a PRD downstream of UX-design conversation.
- **"Fast" caption speed (~2s) is captured.** Brief: *"translated in under three seconds"* and *"Captions appear within ~2 seconds of speech."* PRD §1 carries "~2 seconds" and FR-13/FR-14/FR-15/SM-4 give the latency budget (<2s ASR final, <1s translation, <300ms data channel, median <2.5s end-to-end). Faithful and operationalized.
- **Two-users-only / no-market-product framing is faithful.** §2.3 Non-Users, §9 Non-Goals, §10.1 In Scope, and §11 SM-C1 (counter-metric against DAU optimization) all reinforce the Brief's "two specific people" framing. Multiple independent reinforcement points — robust.
- **The OS-sandbox / "own the call" reasoning from the Brief is preserved.** PRD §1 paragraph 2 carries the "buildable answer is to own the call itself" framing nearly verbatim.
- **The "free forever at this scale" / $0 cost commitment is captured as a hard requirement.** Brief §3 names it; PRD §6.2 makes it a hard requirement with budget alerts and a killswitch. Operationalized.
- **The counter-metrics (§11 SM-C1/C2/C3) are a PRD-level addition that protects the Brief's intent.** SM-C2 ("Do not optimize latency at the cost of accuracy") explicitly protects the Brief's qualitative-conversation goal from being optimized away by FR-level latency targets. SM-C1 protects against DAU-thinking creeping into a 2-person app. These are PRD-native additions that *defend* Brief intent rather than contradict it.
